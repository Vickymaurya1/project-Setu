FOUNDED IN A SINGLE MOHALLA CLASSROOM, GHAZIABAD

Setu started with six plastic chairs, one borrowed Android phone, and a question a shopkeeper asked us: "Beta, yeh UPI cheez kya hai?" ("What even is this UPI thing?")

We realised the digital divide in small-town India isn't about access anymore — most households already own a smartphone. It's about confidence. People can own a device for years and still be afraid to send a text without help, terrified of scams, locked out of government portals meant to serve them.

So we built Setu around one idea: sit next to someone, on their terms, in their language, until the fear turns into fluency. No jargon. No English-only manuals. Just patient, repeatable teaching — run by local youth we train and pay as facilitators.

Today we work across 14 neighbourhoods in Ghaziabad, Meerut, and East Delhi, in partnership with local schools, ration shops, and resident welfare associations who lend us a room for two hours a week.

What we run

Four programs, one bridge

Each program targets a different point where people get stuck between the offline world they know and the online world they're pushed into.

01 / Elders

Smartphone Sahayata

Weekly sit-down sessions for people 55+, covering calls, video chat with family abroad, and spotting scam messages before they cost anything.

Ongoing · weekly
02 / Small business

Kirana Connect

We help local shopkeepers set up UPI, list on delivery apps, and keep digital stock ledgers — without needing to trust a third-party "agent" who charges hidden fees.

6-week cohort
03 / Youth

Yuva Digital Skills

Job-readiness for 18–24 year olds: filling government forms, writing a professional email, using spreadsheets, and spotting fake job postings.

8-week cohort
04 / Community

Mohalla Digital Camps

One-day pop-up camps in a park or community hall — walk-in help for anything from Aadhaar updates to setting up a first email address.

Monthly · drop-in
